Designing a Smarter Way to Shop for Gaming Gear

Yeny is a user-centered gaming gear marketplace that simplifies the buying process for gamers by providing advanced filtering tools and international purchasing solutions.

Duration

3 Weeks

Course

Google UX Design Certificate

Role

UX & UI Designer

The Problem

Many professional and aspiring gamers struggle to find the right gaming equipment because online stores provide limited product information. Additionally, users in some regions cannot access new gaming products released abroad due to customs restrictions or delayed availability in their country.

Pain Points

Limited Product Information

Users struggle to understand which gaming gear fits their needs because most online stores only provide basic specs without deeper technical details.

Difficulty Comparing Products

Many gamers want to compare products based on specific technical features, but existing sites don't offer sufficient filters or comparison tools.

Restricted Access to Global Products

Users are frustrated that new gaming equipment released abroad isn't available in their country for months or years.

Lack of Guidance

Beginners often don't know what to look for in gaming gear and need clearer information to make confident purchasing decisions.

Understanding the User

Through qualitative user research, I identified two primary personas representing different user segments.

Persona: Alp Kaya

Profile: Software developer and competitive gamer

Need: Find high-performance gaming equipment because his current mouse is sluggish and costs him wins in games.

Persona: Can Demir

Profile: Tech-savvy student in Istanbul

Need: Buy newly released tech gear from other countries because it's not available locally and importers charge too much.

Usability Study Findings

I conducted an unmoderated usability study with 5 participants in Turkey over 10-20 minutes each. Key findings informed design iterations.

Filter Panel Visibility

Users found the detailed filtering panel helpful but said it was slightly hard to notice at first glance on the homepage.

Make the filter panel more prominent or add an onboarding tooltip to highlight its features.

Too Many Filter Options

Some users felt overwhelmed by too many filter categories appearing at the same time.

Group related filters and use collapsible sections for a cleaner layout.

Missing Sorting Options

Participants wanted clearer sorting choices (by price, popularity, newest release) in addition to filtering.

Add a sticky "Sort by" menu near the top of the results page for better control.

Design Approach

The design prioritizes clarity, accessibility, and intuitive navigation for both desktop and mobile users.

Clear Visual Hierarchy

Established strong information hierarchy to guide users' attention and ensure key content stands out clearly.

Color & Contrast

Carefully selected blue color theme to evoke trust, with WCAG AA contrast standards for accessibility.

Legible Typography

Used sans-serif typefaces for better readability on both desktop and mobile screens.

Familiar Patterns

Interface follows common e-commerce conventions, helping users navigate intuitively based on familiar mental models.

Clean Design

Minimalist layout reduces visual noise and supports cognitive ease.

Gestalt Principles

Applied proximity, similarity, and alignment to enhance visual grouping and user flow.

Key Takeaways

Impact: Yeny empowers gamers to make smarter buying decisions and access global gaming products without barriers.

What I Learned

  • The importance of conducting user research to uncover real pain points
  • How detailed features like filtering can remain simple through good UX design
  • The value of accessibility and visual hierarchy in building intuitive interfaces
  • How continuous user feedback drives iteration and improves the final product

Next Steps

Designer Collaboration

Full Responsiveness

Develop a fully responsive version for seamless experience across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.
